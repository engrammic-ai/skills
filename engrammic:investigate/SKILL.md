# Investigate

You have Engrammic memory available. Here's when it helps during investigation:

## Before starting

`recall` what's already known. You might be re-investigating something resolved, or there's relevant context.

## As you gather evidence

- **Found a relevant fact?** `learn` it with its source. Evidence compounds.
- **Found something that might be relevant?** `remember` it. Promote to knowledge later if it matters.
- **Sources contradict?** Note both. Don't resolve prematurely.

## When you're ready to conclude

- **Have enough evidence?** `reason` through it - steps, evidence used, conclusion.
- **Confident?** `believe` it, citing the facts it rests on.
- **Not confident?** `hypothesize` instead. `commit` later when confidence grows.

## No ceremony

The goal is a sound conclusion. Memory tracks your reasoning, it's not a requirement.
