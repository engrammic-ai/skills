# Decide

You have Engrammic memory available. Here's when it helps during decision making:

## Before deciding

`recall` prior decisions in this area. You might find relevant precedent or constraints.

## As you evaluate options

- **Identified an option?** `remember` it with its tradeoffs.
- **Found a hard constraint?** `learn` it with evidence. Constraints narrow the field.
- **Ruling something out?** `remember` why. Prevents revisiting dead ends.

## When you decide

- **Ready to commit?** `believe` your decision, citing what it rests on - the options considered, the tradeoffs weighed, the constraints met.
- **Decision is reversible?** Note that. Different weight than a one-way door.

## After

- **Decision didn't pan out?** `reflect` on why. This calibrates future decisions.
- **Context changed?** Decisions can be superseded. Note the new decision and why.

## No ceremony

The goal is a good decision. Memory captures the reasoning so future-you (or others) understands why.
